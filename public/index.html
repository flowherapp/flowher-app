Know Your Rights
Plain-language ADA facts
▼
You don't have to disclose your diagnosis. You can request accommodations by describing how your condition affects your work — without naming it.
ADHD, autism, anxiety, and sensory processing disorders all qualify as disabilities under the ADA for companies with 15+ employees.
Reasonable accommodations can include: written instructions, extended deadlines, reduced noise environments, flexible start times, or a private workspace.
Your employer cannot retaliate against you for requesting an accommodation. If they do, that is an EEOC violation.
You can request accommodations at any time — not just at hiring. There is no deadline for asking.
